   ‚Ä¢ Frequency illusion (Baader-Meinhof effect): The sensation that something recently noticed appears everywhere.

   ‚Ä¢ Framing bias: Judgment influenced by how information is presented (e.g., "90% success" vs. "10% failure").

   ‚Ä¢ Retrieval-induced forgetting: Remembering certain details may cause other information to be forgotten.
   ‚Ä¢ Emotional congruence bias: Memory affected by the emotional state at recall.
   ‚Ä¢ False memory bias: Involuntary creation of inaccurate or altered memories.

   ‚Ä¢ Optimism bias: Overestimating the likelihood of success.
   ‚Ä¢ Pessimism bias: Overestimating the likelihood of failure.

   ‚Ä¢ Loss aversion bias: Fearing losses more than valuing equivalent gains.
   ‚Ä¢ Sunk cost fallacy: Continuing an action solely because resources have already been invested.

   ‚Ä¢ Outgroup homogeneity bias: Perceiving members of other groups as more similar than those in one‚Äôs own group.
